Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

Our Commitment to Privacy

At Shukla & Co. Chartered Accountants, we are committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal and financial information. As a professional accounting firm regulated by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI), we adhere to the highest standards of professional conduct and data protection.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or engage our professional services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

When you engage our services or contact us, we may collect:

  • Name, email address, phone number, and mailing address
  • Business information (company name, industry, revenue)
  • Tax identification numbers (PAN, TAN, GST registration)
  • Financial information (bank account details, income statements, balance sheets)
  • Employment information (salary details, investment declarations)

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect:

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Referring website addresses
  • Device information and operating system

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Professional Services: To provide accounting, tax, audit, and advisory services
  • Compliance: To comply with legal, regulatory, and professional obligations
  • Communication: To respond to inquiries and provide client support
  • Billing: To process payments and maintain financial records
  • Service Improvement: To enhance our services and website functionality
  • Marketing: To send newsletters and service updates (with your consent)

3. Professional Confidentiality

As chartered accountants, we are bound by strict professional confidentiality obligations under the Chartered Accountants Act, 1949, and the Code of Ethics issued by ICAI.

We will never:

  • Disclose your information to third parties without your explicit consent (except as required by law)
  • Use your information for purposes other than providing professional services
  • Sell, rent, or trade your personal information

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

4.1 Legal Obligations

  • Tax authorities (Income Tax Department, GST authorities, etc.)
  • Regulatory bodies (ICAI, MCA, SEBI, RBI, etc.)
  • Law enforcement agencies (when legally required)

4.2 Service Providers

  • Cloud storage providers (with encryption and security measures)
  • Payment processors (for billing and invoicing)
  • IT service providers (under strict confidentiality agreements)

4.3 With Your Consent

We may share information with your banks, financial institutions, or other professionals (lawyers, consultants) only with your explicit written consent.

5. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: All data transmission uses SSL/TLS encryption
  • Access Controls: Limited access to authorized personnel only
  • Secure Storage: Encrypted cloud storage with regular backups
  • Physical Security: Locked cabinets and restricted office access
  • Regular Audits: Periodic security assessments and updates
  • Employee Training: Staff trained on data protection and confidentiality

6. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide ongoing professional services
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements (typically 8-10 years for tax records)
  • Resolve disputes and enforce agreements
  • Maintain professional audit trails

After the retention period, we securely destroy or anonymize your information.

7. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: Request copies of your personal information
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Restriction: Request restriction of processing
  • Portability: Request transfer of your data to another service provider
  • Objection: Object to processing for marketing purposes
  • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for optional data processing

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@shuklaandco.in

8. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
  • Improve website functionality and user experience

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

9. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (government portals, regulatory authorities, etc.). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of significant changes by posting a notice on our website or sending you an email. The "Last Updated" date at the top indicates when this policy was last revised.
